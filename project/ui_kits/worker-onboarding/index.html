Designer's notes
v2 · reconciled with real compliance
What this version adds
v1 was too aggressive — it deferred genuinely mandatory compliance. v2 keeps the motivation engine but folds your real required steps back in, each framed around why it gets you to a shift.
1 · Résumé AI express lane
Kept your "Accelerate your onboarding" upload. It parses past experience (with references), certifications & education — then surfaces them in Boost to review, instead of forcing them as upfront form-fields.
2 · Casual contract + e-signature
Now a required sub-step under "Right to work & contract": the Casual On-Hire agreement, the three statements to acknowledge, and a real draw-to-sign signature pad. Signed state shows a dated receipt.
3 · MyGig Wallet consent
Explicit, required acceptance to create the Wallet — framed as the thing that enables next-day pay — with PayID/bank payout underneath.
4 · Mandatory tax declaration
Full ATO payee declaration — TFN, residency, tax-free threshold, study/training loans, SFSS, and the true-and-correct declaration. Gated before super.
5 · Flare super + manual fallback
Flare-powered featured funds with "choose another fund," honest "powered by Flare" attribution and a compact disclaimer — plus the "Having issues? Use the manual form" path that captures fund name, member no., ABN & USI.
What carried over from v1
The reframe to "your first shift," 3 honest milestones with real progress, the FOMO engine (locked shifts that unlock on completion), deferring truly-optional extras to Boost with a strength meter, and full MyGig brand polish.
Clickable prototype — try the résumé upload, the citizen/visa branch, drawing your signature, the wallet consent, the tax form, and Flare's manual-fallback toggle. Progress saves as you go.
